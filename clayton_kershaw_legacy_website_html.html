Clayton Kershaw

Legacy, highlights, and community work from one of baseball's most accomplished left-handed pitchers.

About

Clayton Kershaw has been one of the defining pitchers of his generation. This page summarizes his most important achievements on the mound and the ways he has given back outside baseball.

Quick facts

  • Position: Left-handed starting pitcher
  • Team: Los Angeles Dodgers (career-long)
  • Highlights: Multiple Cy Young Awards, ERA titles, All-Star selections

Why he matters

  1. Consistent excellence: Kershaw combined elite command with dominant secondary pitches for many seasons.
  2. Historical achievements: multiple Cy Youngs, ERA titles, and a no-hitter among his accomplishments.
  3. Community leadership: active in charity work and recognized for sportsmanship.

Legacy as a Pitcher

Pitching style

Kershaw is known for his curveball, excellent command, and ability to change speeds. He used deception and movement more than pure fastball velocity to dominate hitters.

Career highlights

  • Three-time Cy Young Award winner
  • Multiple ERA titles and All-Star selections
  • No-hitter on June 18, 2014

Career Timeline

  1. 2008 — MLB debut with the Los Angeles Dodgers.
  2. 2011 — Breakout season: Cy Young, ERA title, and other awards.
  3. 2014 — NL MVP and no-hitter in June.
  4. 2010s–2020s — Sustained excellence and community leadership off the field.

Charity & Community

Kershaw and his family have supported multiple charitable causes. He co-founded Kershaw's Challenge, an organization that funds projects related to children and families in need.
